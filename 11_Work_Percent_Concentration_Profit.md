## 1. Work Rate (working together)

> 📖 *Source: AOPS Prealgebra §7.6 "Other Rates" — work as a rate (job per unit time); the fence-painting "add the rates" problem.*

- **A rate is just "how much per one unit of time".** Speed is distance per hour; a work rate is jobs per hour. Find each worker's per-hour (or per-day) amount, then combine.
- **Let the whole job = 1:** treat the whole task (a wall, a report, filling a tank) as **1**.
  - If A finishes alone in **n** days, then A does **1/n** of the job each day. This is A's **rate**.
  - Example: A paints a wall in 4 days → A's rate = **1/4** per day.
- **Add the rates:** when people work together, **add their rates**.
  - A's rate + B's rate + … = **total rate** per day.
  - Example: A = 1/4, B = 1/6 → together = 1/4 + 1/6 = **5/12** per day.
- **Time = 1 ÷ total rate:** to find how long the whole job takes, flip the total rate over.
  - Example: total rate 5/12 → time = 12/5 = **2.4 days**.

> 💡 **Competition point:** Rates **add**, times do **not**. Never average the two times. Always change each time into a rate first, add, then flip back (→ Mock Q2-30 below).

## 2. Percentage

> 📖 *Source: AOPS Prealgebra Ch.8 "Percents" — §8.1 (percent = per 100), §8.2 (word problems, "what percent of"), §8.3 (percent increase/decrease).*

- **Percent means "out of 100".** 25% = 25/100 = 0.25. **Handy conversions:** 10% = 1/10, 25% = ¼, 50% = ½, 75% = ¾, 20% = 1/5.
- **"What percent is A of B?"** = **(A ÷ B) × 100%**. This is the *reverse* direction — you're finding the rate, not applying it.
  - Example: 12 out of 48 → 12/48 = 0.25 = **25%**.
- **Increase by a rate:** new value = **x × (1 + rate)**.
  - Example: 200 up 15% → 200 × 1.15 = **230**.
- **Decrease by a rate:** new value = **x × (1 − rate)**.
  - Example: 200 down 20% → 200 × 0.80 = **160**.
- **"n% more than x" vs "n% of x" — not the same!** "40% *of* 50" = 20, but "40% *more than* 50" = 50 × 1.40 = **70**. Read the wording carefully.
- **"What percent change from A to B?"** = **(B − A) ÷ A × 100%** — always over the **original** A. (A **100% increase = doubling**.)
  - Example: 40 → 50 is (50−40)/40 = 25% **increase**; 50 → 40 is (50−40)/50 = 20% **decrease** (different because the base changed).
- **Successive changes — multiply, don't add:** apply each change one after another.
  - Example: up 15% **then** down 20% → x × 1.15 × 0.80 = **x × 0.92** (a 8% drop overall, not 5%).

> ⚠️ **Warning (the book's "Bogus Solution"):** a percent must be taken over the **right whole**. If 60 of 200 are girls, the girls are 60/200 = **30%**, *not* 60/(200−60) = 42.9%. Always divide by the correct total.

> 💡 **Competition point:** "Up 15% then down 20%" is **not** "down 5%." The two percents are taken from **different totals**, so you must multiply the factors (→ Mock Q1-18 below).

## 3. Concentration (salt water / mixtures) — *Sec 1*

> 📖 *Source: AOPS Prealgebra §8.2 (Percent word problems, Problem 8.14 — the oil/vinegar salad-dressing mixture, computing the % of the whole).*

- **Basic formula:** concentration = **(salt ÷ solution) × 100%**.
  - "Solution" = salt + water (the whole liquid).
  - Example: 20 g salt in 80 g water → solution = 100 g → 20/100 = **20%**.
- **Mixing two liquids:** the mixed concentration = **(total salt) ÷ (total solution)**.
  - Add up all the salt on top, add up all the liquid on the bottom, then divide.
- **Add water:** salt stays the **same**, solution goes **up** → concentration goes **down**.
- **Add salt:** salt **and** solution both go up → concentration goes **up**.

> 💡 **Competition point:** In mixture problems, always track the **amount of salt (or alcohol)** separately from the total liquid. The pure part is what stays fixed when you add water (→ AP 2025 Q03 below).

## 4. Profit & Loss

> 📖 *Source: standard retail word-problem framing (cost / marked / selling). AOPS Prealgebra doesn't have a profit chapter, but its §8.2–8.3 give the pieces: discount = percent decrease, markup = percent increase, plus sales tax and commission.*

- **Marked price (label price):** the price on the tag.
  - Marked price = **cost × (1 + profit rate)** when the shop plans a profit on top of cost.
- **Selling price after a discount:** selling price = **marked price × (1 − discount)**.
  - Example: marked $200, 10% off → 200 × 0.90 = **$180**.
- **Profit:** profit = **selling price − cost**. If this is negative, it is a **loss**.

> 💡 **Competition point:** Keep the three prices clear — **cost**, **marked price**, **selling price**. Most trick questions swap them. Write each as a formula before solving (→ NM 2010 Q21 below).

## 5. Average, Median & Mode

> 📖 *Source: AOPS Prealgebra §13.1 "Basic Statistics" — mean/median/mode/range, and the "balance" view of the average.*

- **Mean (average) = total ÷ count**, so **total = average × count.** This one line solves most average problems.
  - Example: average 80 over 5 tests → total = 80 × 5 = **400**.
- **Median = the middle value** once the numbers are **sorted** (for an even count, the average of the two middle ones). **Mode = the value that appears most often.** **Range = largest − smallest.**
  - Example: 3, 7, 7, 9, 20 → mean = 46/5 = 9.2, median = **7**, mode = **7**, range = 20 − 3 = **17**. (Note the one big value 20 pulls the *mean* up but not the *median* — that's why median resists outliers.)
- **The "balance" idea:** the average is the balance point — the total amount **above** it equals the total amount **below** it. Adding the **same amount to every value** shifts the mean by exactly that amount.
- **When one value changes:** the average shifts by **(change in that value) ÷ count**.
  - Example: one score goes up by 6, with 12 students → average rises by 6 ÷ 12 = **0.5**.
- **Going backwards:** if the average moved by a known amount, then **count = (change in the value) ÷ (change in average)**.

> 💡 **Competition point:** A single correction moves the average by (difference ÷ number of people). Turn that around to **find the number of people**: number = difference ÷ (average change) (→ Mock Q4-5 below).

## Cheat Sheet

| Idea | Key point |
|------|-----------|
| Whole job | set it equal to 1 |
| Rate of one worker | 1 ÷ (days alone) |
| Working together | add the rates, then flip for time |
| What percent is A of B | (A ÷ B) × 100% |
| Increase / decrease | × (1 + rate) / × (1 − rate) |
| n% more than x | x × (1 + n%), not n% of x |
| % change A→B | (B − A) ÷ A (over the original) |
| Two changes in a row | multiply the factors, don't add |
| Concentration | (pure part ÷ whole liquid) × 100% |
| Mixing liquids | total salt ÷ total solution |
| Profit | selling price − cost |
| Selling after discount | marked × (1 − discount) |
| Mean | total = average × count |
| Median / Mode / Range | middle (sorted) / most common / max − min |
| Average shift | difference ÷ count |

## Quick Check

1. A finishes a job in 6 days, B in 3 days. How long do they take working together?
2. A price of $250 goes up 20%, then down 10%. What is the final price?
3. Mix 100 g of 30% salt water with 100 g of pure water. What is the new concentration?
4. A toy costs $80. The marked price is cost plus 25%, then it is sold at 10% off. What is the profit?
5. Correcting one student's mark by +12 raised the class average by 0.5. How many students are there?
6. In a test, 18 of 24 students passed. What percent passed?
7. Find the mean, median, mode and range of: 4, 8, 8, 10, 15.

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. Rates: 1/6 + 1/3 = 1/6 + 2/6 = 3/6 = 1/2 per day → time = **2 days**
2. 250 × 1.20 × 0.90 = 300 × 0.90 = **$270**
3. Salt = 30 g, total liquid = 200 g → 30/200 = **15%**
4. Marked = 80 × 1.25 = 100; selling = 100 × 0.90 = 90; profit = 90 − 80 = **$10**
5. Number = 12 ÷ 0.5 = **24 students**
6. 18/24 = 0.75 = **75%**
7. total 45 → mean = 45/5 = **9**; sorted 4,8,8,10,15 → median = **8**; mode = **8**; range = 15 − 4 = **11**

</details>
